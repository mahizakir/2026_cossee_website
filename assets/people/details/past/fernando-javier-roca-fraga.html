Javier did his PhD at the Massey University in Palmerston North, New Zealand. He is originally from Ecuador and completed a Bachelor of Agricultural Engineering at Zamorano University in Honduras. He moved to New Zealand to pursue postgraduate studies in pastoral livestock production. His work with Shinichi involves both meta-analytical studies as well as field work to investigate the effect of maternal pregnancy nutrition on sheep productivity.

Javier works as a lecturer at the Massey University in Palmerston North, New Zealand.
