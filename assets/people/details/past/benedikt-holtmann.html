Originally from Germany, Benedikt moved to New Zealand to do his PhD with Shinichi. In his doctoral research, he examined the causes and consequences of behavioural variation using the dunnock (Prunella modularis) as a study species. His work combined field and laboratory work as well as meta-analytical approaches.

Benedikt is back in Germany working as a postdoctoral researcher.
