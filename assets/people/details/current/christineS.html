My research focuses on quantitative methods in paleoecology, new methods of extracting information from the fossil record, and patterns of extinction in insects. I joined COSSEE in 2026 to continue and expand upon this work, synthesizing paleontological and macro- and meta-ecological research. I am most interested in the integration of fossils into broad-scale ecological questions as they provide a unique axis – deep time – to our investigations. Currently, I am working on fossilized plant-insect interactions; analyzing how community networks change structure across periods of environmental upheaval, and the implications that such shifts have upon terricolous ecosystems during times of global change.
