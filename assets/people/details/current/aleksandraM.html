Aleksandra Milenović is a first year PhD student of Biological Sciences, and is working as a Teaching Assistant for Introduction to Biological Diversity (BIOL108). Her thesis is in conceptualisation stage, but is envisioned to examine the processes of big-team science, particularly in the context of interdisciplinary collaboration. She is also a member of the University College Dublin Media and Entertainment Psychology Lab and is a member of the Advisory Board for the European Research Council Funded Project, "Investigating Fictional Representations of Child Sexual Abuse in Contemporary Culture: Myths and Understanding" (CSAReps). Prior to her membership in COSSEE, she worked as a Research Assistant within the CSAReps project, co-authoring the project outputs as well as co-organising the project's dissemination strands. Her interdisciplinary and international education consists of a Cum Laude Masters of Media Studies from Utrecht University, Netherlands and Bachelor of Science in Psychology, University College Dublin, Ireland.
