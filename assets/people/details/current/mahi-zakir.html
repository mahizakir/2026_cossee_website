I joined COSSEE at the University of Alberta in March 2026. I hold an MSc in Computer Science from the University of Alberta and a BSc in Computer Science and Engineering from BRAC University. I am deeply passionate about machine learning and AI, not just as research topics, but as tools that can genuinely change how people access and use information. I strongly believe that data should not sit in silos. It should be accessible, understandable, and actionable. My previous works include projects like RT-Pred (a ML system that predicts chromatographic retention time across methods to improve compound identification) and RADOR (an AI powered rare disease assistant that integrates structured knowledge and language models to support diagnostic reasoning). Now at COSSEE, I explore how large language models and ML can aid in meta-analysis and synthesis of Open Science data in Ecology and Evolution. By automatically extracting, organising, and reasoning over published researches, these tools can help identify patterns, generate insights, and support evidence-based conclusions at a scale that would be very hard manually. Outside of research, I am a big fan of pop culture and keeping up with global trends. I believe the best ideas often emerge when different worlds collide.
