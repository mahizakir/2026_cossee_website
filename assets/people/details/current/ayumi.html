I am a behavioural ecologist interested in ecological and evolutionary patterns in birds. I use phylogenetic comparative methods and evidence synthesis to study how behaviour, morphology, and environmental variation are linked across species. I am particularly interested in carefully re-examining widely accepted hypotheses and rules, asking when they hold, how robust they are, and whether the ways we test them are appropriate. Outside research, I enjoy going for walks, letting my mind wander through different ideas, and taking in the scenery.
