I work as an Associate Professor at the University of Alberta in Edmonton, Canada, leading a sister lab to COSSEE, called I-DEEL (Inter-Disciplinary Ecology and Evolution Lab).

I am a biologist with interdisciplinary expertise spanning ecotoxicology, ecology, evolution, environmental science, biomedical, and social research. I specialize in research synthesis methodologies, including systematic reviews, evidence maps, and meta-analysis, to address fundamental questions in maternal effects, aging, sexual dimorphism, and evolutionary processes. My peer-reviewed publications examine these topics through quantitative lenses, and I also develop and refine methods to tackle pervasive challenges in scientific practice, such as data non-independence, heterogeneity, allometric scaling, and reporting biases.

In addition to many meta-analyses, I conduct meta-research on transparency, equity, diversity, and inclusion within the research ecosystem. By evaluating research practices, advancing reporting guidelines, and assessing recognition structures such as awards and authorship norms, I strive to foster more rigorous, open, and socially inclusive science. Through collaborative projects, both local and international, I aim to catalyse systemic improvements in how research is designed, conducted, and disseminated, helping to build a more transparent, equitable, and resilient scientific community.
